  Food courts are dining areas with multiple food vendors, where customers order food at the counter and then eat it in a shared space. Food court content typically includes a variety of cuisines and choices, such as: Fast-food chains: Common in North American and European food courts, these include McDonald's, Sbarro, and Panda Express Independent vendors: These may offer local cuisine, such as in Asian and African food courts Global choices: Larger food courts may offer more global choices

  Food types:
Food courts offer a wide range of cuisines, including fast food, North Indian, South Indian, Chinese, and Arabian. Larger food courts may offer more global choices.
  Dining experience:
Food courts are self-serve, with customers ordering meals at the vendors and then taking them to a shared dining area. Some food courts may also offer takeout.
